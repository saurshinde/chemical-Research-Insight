Corn silage inoculants are specialized additives that contain anaerobic lactic acid bacteria (LAB) and are designed to improve the fermentation process of silage. Silage refers to crops, such as corn, grass, and alfalfa, that are fermented and stored for livestock feed. Inoculants are primarily used to enhance the microbial activity during the fermentation process, leading to improved fermentation efficiency, better preservation, and higher feed quality.

These inoculants work by boosting the growth of beneficial bacteria, which are involved in the fermentation process, while inhibiting harmful bacteria that can lead to spoilage. This process helps create a more stable silage product with increased nutritional value and digestibility for livestock.

In the corn silage market, inoculants are typically classified into two categories based on the type of bacteria they contain: heterofermentative and homofermentative. These classifications refer to the metabolic processes by which the bacteria in the inoculants ferment sugars, contributing to the production of lactic acid, which helps preserve the silage.
